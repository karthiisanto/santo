Kerala bus layout — 2 left + 3 right

Most Kerala KSRTC and private buses have 2 seats on the left side and 3 on the right. The extra seat on the right (3-seat side) means more passengers face the sun when it comes from the east (morning) or west (afternoon). The shaded side is almost always the better pick for window seats. Rows 3–8 are the best zone — front rows catch windshield glare, rear rows get engine heat.

District direction reference

Why this works for local routes

Local city and town buses in Kerala change direction often — you may not know if you're heading north or south. Nizhal uses the actual sun position calculated from the current time and Kerala's latitude (≈10°N) to determine which compass direction the sun is in. It then shows you which side of the bus — regardless of its direction — gets more direct sun right now, and recommends the shaded side.

Quick boarding tip

When you board any local bus, glance at the sun through the windows. The side where you can see the sun directly is the sunny side — move to the opposite side. At midday (10am–2pm), the sun is nearly overhead so both sides are similar; pick an aisle seat in the middle rows instead.
